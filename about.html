About Noah

The Story

Noah Santella is a silversmith and stone-setter who makes every piece by hand, from raw sterling silver to the finished clasp. What began as a fascination with the way light moves inside a cut stone grew into a full studio practice - designing, forging, and finishing one-of-a-kind jewelry to order.

He works almost entirely in sterling silver, drawn to stones with real depth and movement - azurite, labradorite, and other natural cabochons that shift and flash as they catch the light. Every bezel and rope frame is cut, shaped, and burnished by hand, so no two pieces are ever quite alike.

Alongside original commissions, Noah brings tired and broken jewelry back to life - resetting lost stones, rebuilding worn settings, mending chains and clasps, and recutting dull gems on professional lapidary equipment. Much of his work comes from clients who return year after year, and from the people they send his way.
